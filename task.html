Golden Temple


The Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, is the holiest gurdwara (Sikh temple) and a prominent spiritual center for Sikhs worldwide. Located in Amritsar, Punjab, India, it's renowned for its stunning architecture and gold-plated exterior. The temple is built around the Amrit Sarovar (holy pool) and is a place of worship open to all, regardless of background.The temple is built on a slightly lower level than the surrounding land, symbolizing humility and equality. It features a unique Sikh architectural style with four entrances, signifying openness and inclusivity for people from all walks of life.The Golden Temple is the central place of worship for Sikhs, housing the Sri Guru Granth Sahib, their holy scripture.
